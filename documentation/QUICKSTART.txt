
Quickstart:
=============
		
1) Solder in place the capacitors:	 

	2 x 100 uF     (Make sure the polarity/orientation is as indicated on the board)


2) Solder in place the pin headers:

	2xpin  = 5V, GND               (power pins, battery preferred i.e. a single 18650 or two 18650 in parallel)

	4xpin  = IN0, IN1, IN2, IN3    (ADC input pins)


3) Prepare SDcard

	Take an empty micro SDcard (Standard formatting: FAT) and copy the configuration file "phcfg.txt"  to it.

	   https://github.com/phyclopsphyclops/PhyclopsOnev2-downloads/blob/main/sdcard_example/phcfg.txt 	   

-----------------------------

4) Power On by connecting GND,5V to your power source.
	The LED will start to flash once every 5-8 seconds.
	
5) Insert SDcard
    The	LED will stop flashing and be permanently on or off for about 10 sec, followed by a short flicker.
        
6) Recording in progress
	The LED will flash approximately once every 15 seconds while data is being recorded.
	
7) Recover data
	Simply remove the SDcard and read/process the recorded data with the provided scripts:
	
		https://github.com/phyclopsphyclops/PhyclopsOnev2-downloads/tree/main/R
		https://github.com/phyclopsphyclops/PhyclopsOnev2-downloads/tree/main/python
		
	
8) Continue recording
	Press the RESET button (blue) on the board. 
	Re-insert the SDcard as in Step 5) and the recording will continue.
	(Files on the SDcard are *never* overwritten or deleted by the device so data cannot be lost unless files are removed manually)
	Note that all timestamps recorded are *relative* to the time of last power-on or time of last reset.
		
9) Safe shutdown
	Remove the SDcard
	Disconnect from power
	



Battery power:							   		Max. Runtime
==============================================================
	2x 18650 4.2V (parallel, 4000mAh)				19 days
	1x 18650 4.2V (2000mAh)         				10 days
	3x AA 4.5V    (series, ~2000 mAh)                tbd

NOTE: There is no overdischarge protection, so in order to avoid damage to the SDcard it is recommended to
      shutdown the device and recharge/renew batteries *before* the maximum runtime is reached.
